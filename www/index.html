Sentiment Analysis of Classic Literature

Sentiment analysis measures the polarity of a piece of text. In this context, polarity refers to the feelings conveyed by thetext, where a positive polarity is indicates "good" feelings (e.g. excitement, arousal, laughter) and a negative polarity indicates "bad" feelings (e.g. anger, sadness, jealousy). Select one of the great works of literature below to see a its sentiment analysis on a per-sentence basis. The score shown represents a moving mean taken over a window of 100 sentences in width.

The sentiment score for a piece of text was calculated as the sum of the word scores. The word scores were taken from the AFINN lexicon (Nielsen 2011). A score of 0 was assigned to words absent from the lexicon.
